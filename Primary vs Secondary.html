üìö Learning Objectives

üî¨ Primary Data

Definition: Data collected directly by the researcher for their specific study

Characteristics: First-hand, original, collected for current research purpose

Examples: Surveys you conduct, experiments, interviews, observations

Advantages: Tailored to research needs, current, controlled quality

Disadvantages: Time-consuming, expensive, requires expertise

üìã Secondary Data

Definition: Data previously collected by someone else for a different purpose

Characteristics: Second-hand, existing, reused for new research

Examples: Government statistics, published studies, databases, reports

Advantages: Quick access, cost-effective, large datasets available

Disadvantages: May not fit research needs perfectly, potential quality issues

üéØ Key Questions to Ask:

  • Who collected this data? You/Your team ‚Üí Primary, Someone else ‚Üí Secondary
  • Was it collected for your research? Yes ‚Üí Primary, No ‚Üí Secondary
  • Is this the first use of the data? Yes ‚Üí Primary, No ‚Üí Secondary
  • Did you design the data collection? Yes ‚Üí Primary, No ‚Üí Secondary

Memory Aids:

  • Primary: "Prime" = first, original, direct from source
  • Secondary: "Second" = used again, indirect, borrowed
  • Primary: You are the "primary" collector
  • Secondary: Data has a "secondary" life in your research
